Bun parotta

Bun parotta is a popular Madurai street food that is essentially a cross between a bun and a parotta. It is made by folding the parotta into the shape of a bun, similar to a Malabar parotta. Karupanna and his wife Vijayam claim to have been the first to make bun parotta in 1991 at their stall Madurai Bun Parotta Kada. Bun parotta is typically served with hot curries.The bun parotta has the shape of a bun and is also fluffier than the usual Malabar parotta. Although the ingredients are almost the same in both kinds of parotta, the bun parotta is said to be made without eggs, making it a good option for vegetarians.

Ingredients

  • 3 cup maida.
  • 1 tbsp sugar.
  • ¼ tsp baking soda.
  • ½ tsp salt.
  • milk, for kneading.
  • ¼ cup oil, for soaking.

Directions

  1. Take salt, sugar, water in a bowl and mix well.
  2. Take eggs in a bowl, add water and oil and mix well.
  3. Add flour and mix well.
  4. Knead this to a dough.
  5. Now knead this dough for atleast 5 mins till it is soft and strechy.
  6. Apply oil on it and cover with a wet cloth. Let it marinate for 3 to 4 hours.
  7. Now divide the dough into equal balls. Apply oil over it and leave aside for 1 hour.
  8. Take the dough. Flatten it on a oiled work surface using your oiled hands.
  9. Now strech it as much as you can. Fold one side on it, fold other side on it.
  10. Now roll it loosely into a bun like. Check images for understanding.
  11. Now heat oil in a cast iron pan and fry on medium heat on both sides till golden and crispy.
  12. Serve with salna.

firstly, adding sugar helps to give nice golden colour to parotta.

additionally, kneading the dough with milk makes paratha soft.
